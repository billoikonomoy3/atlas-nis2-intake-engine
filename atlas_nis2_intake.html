2 · Size — the target entity (standalone figures)

2003/361/EC. Staff is a hard ceiling (≥). The financial limb is read in the entity's favour: you breach it only by exceeding BOTH turnover AND balance sheet (>).
Medium ≥ 50 staff OR (turnover > €10m AND balance > €10m).   Large ≥ 250 staff OR (turnover > €50m AND balance > €43m).
The trap demonstrates why "or €10m turnover" is negligent: that firm is small / out of scope because its balance sheet is only €5m.

3 · Group structure — Article 6 aggregation

Size is assessed on consolidated figures: +100% of each linked (controlled, >50%) enterprise and a pro-rata share of partner (25–50%) enterprises. A subsidiary of a multinational is never a microenterprise.

4 · Two-year rule (Art 4(2))

A size change only takes effect once a ceiling is crossed in two consecutive accounting periods. A single anomalous year does not flip scope.
No prior band set — the current period's band applies directly.
